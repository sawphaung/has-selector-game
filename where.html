:where() field

Learn CSS's zero-specificity selector by playing — match the right elements AND stay under the specificity budget.

:where() matches exactly like :is() — same list, same elements — with one superpower: it always counts as zero specificity. Even :where(#id, .class) weighs nothing. That's what makes it the backbone of CSS resets and component libraries: styles that apply everywhere but are trivially easy to override. Every level here gives you a budget like (0,1,0) — ids, classes, types — that your selector must not exceed.
